# Rental Management System - User Guide

## 1. System Overview
The Rental Management System is a comprehensive platform for property owners and managers to track real estate portfolios, tenant agreements, and financial performance. It provides automated accounting, structured data management for leases, and comprehensive reporting (Profit & Loss, Statement of Account, Rent Roll).

## 2. Properties
**Purpose:** Track real estate assets, their financial valuations, and associated capital expenditures.
*   **Add Property:** Navigate to the Properties tab. Input the unit details (Highrise/Landed), ownership type (Freehold/Leasehold), and financial valuations (SPA Price, Book Value, Market Value).
*   **Record Tenancy Agreement:** Input the filename or reference of the lease document directly into the property record for easy tracking.
*   **Manage Renovations:** Under a property profile, add renovation records. Input the invoice details, amount paid, and select a depreciation period (1, 3, 5, or 10 years) for automated expense scheduling.

## 3. Tenants
**Purpose:** Maintain a centralized database of tenant contact and identity information.
*   **Add Tenant:** Navigate to the Tenants tab. Input the tenant's legal name, NRIC/Passport number, email, and mobile number. Keep this updated for lease linking.

## 4. Tenancies (Leases)
**Purpose:** Define the contractual relationship between a Property and a Tenant, outlining rental terms and automated deductions.
*   **Create Tenancy:** Navigate to Tenancies. Select an existing Property and Tenant.
*   **Set Rental Terms:** Input the Monthly Gross Rent, Rental Deposit, Surcharge, and Service Fee Deductions. The system calculates the Net Remitted automatically.
*   **Set Deductions:** Input recurring property expenses (Maintenance, Quit Rent, Assessment, Bank Cost of Funds, etc.) to accurately calculate property profitability.
*   **Track Dates:** Input Commencement, Key Collection, Move-In, and Expiration dates.
*   **Close Early:** Use the "Close Early" function to terminate an active lease before its expiration date. This updates the status and logs a termination activity.

## 5. Financial Transactions
**Purpose:** Log daily financial operations to populate accounting reports.
*   **Rent Collections:** Log expected vs. actual rent payments. Record the amount collected, Service/Admin fees, SST, and the date the net rent was remitted to the owner.
*   **Deposit Transactions:** Track deposit lifecycles. Log deposits as Received, Refunded, Applied (to outstanding rent), or Forfeited.
*   **Expense Transactions:** Log ad-hoc or recurring expenses (Utility, Fire Insurance, Maintenance). Ensure invoices are referenced for audit trails.
*   **Monthly Incomes & Expenses:** Use the bulk entry features to record gross rental incomes and categorical expenses grouped by month.

## 6. Tenant Activities
**Purpose:** Maintain an audit log of tenant interactions.
*   **Log Activity:** Add entries for Rent Collections, Payment Reminders, Renewal Negotiations, General Notes, or Terminations.

## 7. Reports & Analytics
**Purpose:** Generate automated financial and operational statements.
Navigate to the Reports tab and select a report type. Set the required "As Of" date or "Period Month" to filter the data.
*   **Statement of Account:** Generates a ledger of all charges and payments for a specific tenancy.
*   **Profit & Loss (P&L):** Displays gross revenue minus all direct/indirect expenses and depreciation, yielding net profit for the portfolio or specific properties.
*   **Monthly Cash Collection:** Tracks actual cash inflow against expected rent.
*   **Arrears / Late Collection Aging:** Identifies overdue rent and ages the debt.
*   **Rent Roll & Tenancy Status:** Provides a snapshot of all active/expiring leases and total monthly rental yields.
*   **Deposit Register:** Reconciles all held security deposits.
*   **Expense & Depreciation Schedule:** Amortizes renovation costs over their selected depreciation periods.

## 8. Settings
*   **Expense Categories:** Manage the chart of accounts. Categorize expenses as Direct or Indirect, and mark non-cash expenses (like Depreciation).
